Falafel Burger

Description

Elevate your burger game with this delectable Falafel Burger recipe. It's a brilliant fusion of Middle Eastern flavors and the classic American Burger, creating a wholesome and satisfying meal. These falafel patties are packed with chickpeas, hearbs, and spices, making them a deliciously vegetarian option that even meat lovers will crave. Let's get cooking!

Ingredients

For the Falafel Patties:

  • 2 (15-ounce) cans of chickpeas, drained and rinsed
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1 small red onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour or chickpea flour (for binding)
  • 2-3 tablespoons olive oil (for frying)

For the Tzatziki Sauce:

  • 1 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cucumber, grated and drained
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

For Assembling the Burger:

  • Burger buns of your choice
  • Sliced tomatoes
  • Sliced red onions
  • Lettuce leaves
  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Optional: Feta cheese crumbles

Steps

  1. Prepare the Falafel Patties:

    • In a food processor, combine the chickpeas, parsley, cilantro, red onion, garlic, cumin, coriander, cayenne pepper, salt, and pepper. Pulse until the mixture reaches a coarse, crumbly texture.
    • Transfer the mixture to a bowl and stir in the flour. This helps bind the mixture together.
    • Shape the mixture into 4-6 patties and place them on a baking sheet. Refrigerate for 15-30 minutes to firm them up.

  2. Make the Tzatziki Sauce:

    • In a bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, lemon juice, chopped dill, salt, and pepper. Mix well and refrigerate until ready to use.

  3. Cook the Falafel Patties

    • Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Once hot, add the falafel patties and cook for about 3-4 minutes per side, or until they are crispy and golden brown. You can also bake them in a preheated oven to 375°F (190°C) for 20-25 minutes, flipping halfway through.

  4. Assemble the Falafel Burger:

    • Toast your burger buns if desired. Place a lettuce leaf on the bottom bun, followed by a falafel patty.
    • Add sliced tomatoes, red onions, and cucumbers on top of the falafel patty.
    • Drizzle generous amounts of tzatziki sauce over the veggies.
    • Optionally, crumble some feta cheese on top for an extra burst of flavor.
    • Top with the other half of the burger bun.

  5. Serve and Enjoy:

    • Serve your Falafel Burger with your favorite side dish like sweet potato fries or a fresh salad. Don't forget extra tzatziki sauce for dipping!
